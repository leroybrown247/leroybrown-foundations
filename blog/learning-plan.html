Time to formulate your Learning Plan!

Sprint 5 - CORE

30th of April 2023

1. What is your long-term goal or career pathway?

In terms of long-term goals not much has changed from Sprint-2, I still hope to attain enough skills and knowledge to enter a career as a web developer. In terms of specialisation, I wish to explore this while studying at Dev Academy.

Ultimately, I hope to make a positive impact on people's lives through the software I develop. Whether it's by creating innovative solutions to real-world problems or by improving the user experience of existing products, I want to be able to use my skills to make a meaningful difference. While I don't have a specific destination in mind, I am committed to continually learning and growing in this field to achieve my long-term goals.

2. What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I believe that my strengths in research, strategy, and communication will be valuable during Bootcamp, while my limitations in time management and debugging skills will be areas for improvement. By focusing on improving these skills and building on my strengths, I am confident that I can overcome any challenges that come my way during Bootcamp.

3. What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

One challenge that I anticipate is time management. While I am eager to learn as much as possible during Bootcamp, I recognize the importance of balancing my workload and avoiding burnout. I plan to approach this challenge by creating a realistic schedule that includes dedicated time for learning, practice, and relaxation. By setting achievable goals and prioritising my workload, I believe that I can manage my time effectively and maximise my learning opportunities during Bootcamp.

4. What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to develop my pairing with others and communicate effectively and efficiently within my pairing.

5. What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

In terms of support, I would like creative and constructive criticism. Understanding where I can improve is always important to me.

6. What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

My personal expectations for Bootcamp include gaining a comprehensive understanding of the skills and knowledge necessary to succeed in the industry, building strong connections with my peers and facilitators, and ensuring that the experience is both enjoyable and productive for everyone involved.

To achieve these goals, I recognise the importance of seeking help when needed. I plan to seek help from my peers and facilitators during class time and work collaboratively with my team members during group projects. Additionally, I will make use of online resources and tutorials to supplement my learning and deepen my understanding of complex topics.

In terms of managing my workload, I commit to being disciplined and strategic in my approach. This includes prioritising my tasks, setting achievable goals, and working steadily to complete all tasks within the given timeframe. I will also make time for self-care and self-reflection, recognising the importance of maintaining a healthy work-life balance.

Furthermore, I understand that working productively and safely with other learners, facilitators, and industry/community representatives is essential for a positive experience. To ensure this, I will actively communicate with my team members, participate in group discussions and activities, and be respectful and considerate towards others. By working together and supporting each other, I believe that we can all achieve our goals and have a productive and enjoyable experience during Bootcamp.
